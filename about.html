Meet the team

Ten students from Swinburne University of Technology Sarawak exploring how AI can raise the quality of learning under SDG 4.

Group 10 · MPU3193

Lecturer: Dr. Serit anak Banyan · Campus: Swinburne University of Technology Sarawak

Yuen Kai CHIA

ID: 104400442
Program: Bachelor of Computer Science (AI & CS)
Origin: Miri, Sarawak, Malaysia

Roles: Website lead (HTML/CSS), information architecture, content integration, APA cross‑checking.

Reflection — Cognitive: I moved from thinking that learning equals more reading to valuing better understanding. AI helps me jump to the right concept quickly, but I verify sources, ask for counter‑arguments, and practise explanation so understanding sticks.

Reflection — Affective: I feel optimistic seeing how captions, translation and quick feedback reduce frustration and include more voices. That hope is paired with responsibility to use AI honestly and respectfully.

Reflection — Psychomotor: I built the responsive layout, stitched the case pages and references, and iterated prompts to produce clear, evidence‑based paragraphs.

SIA JING LI

ID: 104399333
Program: Bachelor of Business (Finance)
Origin: Miri, Sarawak, Malaysia

Roles: Campaign metrics & analytics, budget/time planning, references formatting.

Reflection — Cognitive: AI sped up my scanning of long articles into key points and numbers. I learned to check figures against original tables and to track assumptions so we don’t over‑claim.

Reflection — Affective: I’m motivated when AI removes busywork and leaves space for teamwork and presentation practice. It feels more human, not less.

Reflection — Psychomotor: I compiled engagement data, cleaned spreadsheets, and produced succinct captions and APA entries from messy sources.
